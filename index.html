Welcome to My Academic Profile

I am a PhD student at the CAS Institute of Plasma Physics (ASIPP) in Hefei, China, working under the guidance of Prof. Yuanlai Xie. My research is part of the Neutron Beam Injection team, where I focus on the thermal structural design and optimization of calorimeters for neutron beam injection systems. This work is critical for advancing the efficiency and safety of nuclear fusion technologies, particularly in the context of plasma confinement and energy extraction.

EAST- Experimental Advanced Superconducting Tokamak

PhD Thesis Research Methodology

My PhD thesis employs a multidisciplinary approach, combining computational modeling, thermal-structural analysis, and optimization techniques to address the challenges associated with calorimeter design. The methodology involves:

  • Thermal Analysis: Using computational tools to simulate heat transfer and thermal stresses in calorimeter materials under extreme conditions, such as high neutron flux and thermal gradients.
  • Structural Optimization: Applying advanced optimization algorithms to enhance the mechanical integrity and thermal performance of calorimeter components, ensuring they can withstand operational stresses while maintaining accuracy.
  • Finite Element Analysis (FEA): Leveraging FEA software to model and predict the behavior of materials under thermal and mechanical loads, enabling the identification of critical failure points and design improvements.
  • Experimental Validation: Collaborating with experimental teams to validate computational models using real-world data from neutron beam injection systems, ensuring the reliability and accuracy of the designs.

Academic Background

Prior to my PhD, I completed my MSc in Energy and Power Engineering at Tsinghua University, specializing in Nuclear Engineering and Management. My MSc thesis, titled "CFD Modelling and Simulation of Two-Phase Flow in Thermosyphon Material in Passive Heat Decay Removal System in HTGR", provided me with a strong foundation in computational fluid dynamics (CFD) and heat transfer analysis, which I now apply to my current research.

My work aims to contribute to the development of robust and efficient calorimeter systems, ultimately supporting the advancement of nuclear fusion as a sustainable energy source. I am passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical modeling and practical engineering solutions in the field of nuclear energy.
